## How to choose a kayak paddle

### 1. Confirm the basic product facts

The first facts to confirm are length, weight, blade material, shaft material, adjustability, and whether the paddle breaks down for transport. The reviewed product pages currently confirm a stated weight of 26 oz. and describe the paddles as lightweight, durable, and easy to transport.

### 2. Do not choose only by design

Hornet kayak paddles use graphic designs, but design should be the final decision layer. First verify that the paddle's length, construction, weight, and transport features match your use case.

### 3. Match the paddle to the paddler and use case

A kayak angler, recreational paddler, and fitness paddler may care about different things. Weight and comfort matter for longer paddles. Durability and transport matter if the paddle is used frequently or carried between locations.

### 4. Compare designs only after specs are confirmed

At this stage, the fact pages confirm that Making Waves, Blue Scales, and Crush are active Hornet kayak paddles with different designs. The guide should not claim performance differences between these designs unless product data confirms structural differences.
