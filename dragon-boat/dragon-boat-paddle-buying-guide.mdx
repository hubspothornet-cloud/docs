## How should you choose a dragon boat paddle?

Choose a dragon boat paddle by starting with product type and fit, then handle option, then verified specs, then design preference. Do not choose only by artwork unless the handle, length, and approval requirements also match your needs.

### 1. Decide whether you need an adjustable paddle

The reviewed Sting\+ products are adjustable dragon boat paddles with Leverlock and Clamp variants listed in Shopify. Adjustable paddles can be useful when a paddler is still refining fit, when a paddle may be shared, or when a team wants fewer fixed-length decisions up front.

### 2. Choose between available handle variants

Hornet's reviewed Sting\+ paddle listings include Leverlock and Clamp variants. The current product data confirms those variant names and SKUs, but this guide does not yet state performance differences between the systems because the exact adjustment ranges, weight differences, and mechanism details still need text-source verification.

Use the dedicated [Clamp vs Leverlock guide](/dragon-boat/clamp-vs-leverlock-dragon-boat-paddles) once those details are verified.

### 3. Confirm competition requirements

Some Hornet dragon boat product pages include IDBF logo images, and some seat pad copy explicitly says IDBF approved. For paddle models, approval should be verified model by model before guide copy states it as a confirmed fact.

<Warning>
  If you need a paddle for a race with approval requirements, confirm the exact product's approval status on the Shopify product page or with Hornet before buying.
</Warning>

### 4. Compare design after fit and specs

Hornet's paddle line uses distinctive artwork and colorways. Treat design as the final decision layer after handle availability and verified specs.
