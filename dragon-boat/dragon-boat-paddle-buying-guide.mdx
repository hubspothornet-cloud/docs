## How to choose a dragon boat paddle

### 1. Choose the paddle family

Choose Sting\+ graphic paddles when you want a carbon-fiber performance paddle with full-color graphics on both sides. Choose STEALTH when you want Hornet's elite racing-focused paddle family, marbleized carbon construction, and glossy performance finish.

### 2. Choose Clamp or Leverlock

Choose Clamp when you want the lighter reviewed setup, approximately 16 oz, with a 45 in to 51 in adjustable range. Choose Leverlock when you want the Leverlock adjustment system and a 43 in to 51 in adjustable range, with an approximate 20 oz weight.

### 3. Confirm IDBF and race needs

Hornet's reviewed Sting\+ graphic and STEALTH paddle families are IDBF approved under license 3525. Buyers should still confirm any event-specific requirements with their race or team before ordering.

### 4. Choose design last

Hornet paddles are design-forward, but artwork should come after fit, handle type, weight, approval, and current availability.

## FAQs

### Are Hornet dragon boat paddles IDBF approved?

Hornet's reviewed Sting\+ graphic and STEALTH adjustable dragon boat paddle families are IDBF approved under license 3525. Buyers should still confirm event-specific requirements with their race organizer or team.

### What is the difference between Clamp and Leverlock?

Clamp Adjustable models weigh approximately 16 oz and adjust from 45 in to 51 in. Leverlock Adjustable models weigh approximately 20 oz and adjust from 43 in to 51 in.

### Which Hornet paddle is best for high-level racing?

Start with the STEALTH Performance Series when the buyer asks for elite racing intent. STEALTH is built for high-level racing and emphasizes stiffness, responsiveness, durability, and a modern marbleized carbon construction.

### Which Hornet paddle is best for bold artwork?

Start with the Sting\+ graphic paddles when the buyer wants full-color graphics on both sides with verified PMI foam, 3K carbon fiber, Diamond Edge, and IDBF-approved performance specs.
