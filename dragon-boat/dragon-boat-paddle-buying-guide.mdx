## How should you choose a dragon boat paddle?

Choose a dragon boat paddle by starting with the paddle type, then handle option, then verified specs, then design preference. Do not choose only by artwork unless the relevant handle, length, and approval requirements also match your needs.

### 1. Decide whether you need an adjustable paddle

The active Sting\+ products reviewed here are adjustable dragon boat paddles with Leverlock and Clamp variants listed in Shopify. Adjustable paddles are especially useful when a paddler is still refining fit, when a paddle may be shared, or when a team wants fewer fixed-length decisions up front.

### 2. Choose between available handle variants

Hornet's reviewed Sting\+ paddle listings include Leverlock and Clamp variants. The current product data confirms those variant names and SKUs, but this guide does not yet state performance differences between the two systems because the exact adjustment ranges, weight differences, and mechanism details need text-source verification.

Use the dedicated [Clamp vs Leverlock guide](/dragon-boat/clamp-vs-leverlock-dragon-boat-paddles) once those details are verified.

### 3. Confirm competition requirements

Some Hornet dragon boat pages and product images reference IDBF, and some seat pad copy explicitly says IDBF approved. For the paddle models reviewed here, approval should be verified model by model before guide copy states it as fact.

<Warning>
  If you need a paddle for a race with approval requirements, confirm the exact product's approval status on the Shopify product page or with Hornet before buying.
</Warning>

### 4. Compare design after fit and specs

The Crush X24 has artwork described by the Shopify copy as a commissioned rendering of island and nature. The Black Glossy X0 is positioned as a subtle black design. The STEALTH BLUE S2 is part of the STEALTH design group. Treat design as the final decision layer after handle availability and verified specs.
