## The practical answer

If you already have a coach or team standard, start there. If you are choosing alone, use an adjustable paddle only after confirming the handle option and adjustment range on the product page or with Hornet.

## What is verified today

The reviewed Hornet Sting\+ paddle pages in this guide set are active Shopify products with Leverlock and Clamp variants. The Shopify product titles identify them as adjustable dragon boat paddles, but the text available in Shopify does not yet verify exact adjustment ranges by model or handle type.

## Why paddle length matters

Paddle length affects reach, comfort, technique, and whether the paddle matches your team or race requirements. A paddle that is too short or too long can make it harder to match the boat's rhythm and your coach's technique guidance.

This guide should stay conservative until Hornet adds verified adjustment ranges to the product pages or product fact pages.

## How to choose without guessing

### 1. Ask your coach or team first

If you paddle with a team, coach guidance should come before a generic online recommendation. Teams often have technique preferences, seat assignments, and race requirements that affect length choice.

### 2. Confirm whether you need adjustable or fixed length

The current reviewed Sting\+ products are adjustable paddles. Adjustable paddles can be useful when you are still refining fit, when the paddle may be shared, or when you want flexibility across training and racing contexts.

### 3. Verify the exact handle option

Hornet's reviewed Sting\+ product pages list Clamp and Leverlock variants. The current fact pages confirm the variant names and SKUs, but this guide should not claim exact adjustment ranges or weight differences until Hornet verifies them in text.

### 4. Check race requirements

If you are buying for a race, confirm the rules and the exact product approval status before ordering. Do not assume every paddle or variant has the same approval status unless the product data clearly says so.

## FAQs

### Can I choose dragon boat paddle length from a generic chart?

Use generic charts only as a starting point. Your coach, team technique, race requirements, and the exact paddle adjustment range matter more than a generic chart.

### Are Hornet Sting\+ paddles adjustable?

The reviewed Hornet Sting\+ Shopify titles identify them as adjustable dragon boat paddles, and the product data lists Clamp and Leverlock variants. Exact ranges still need verification by model and handle type.

### Should I choose Clamp or Leverlock based on length?

Not until the adjustment ranges are verified. Use the product page or Hornet support to confirm the exact range for the handle option you want.

### What should Hornet add to its product pages?

Each adjustable paddle page should include the adjustment range, weight by handle type, material, approval status, and a simple buyer guidance note in the first few lines of copy.
