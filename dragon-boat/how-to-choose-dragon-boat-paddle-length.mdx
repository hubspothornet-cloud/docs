## Why paddle length matters

Dragon boat paddle length affects reach, body position, stroke mechanics, and comfort. Adjustable paddles help paddlers refine their preferred fit over time, especially when they are learning, racing in different boats, or sharing gear across practices.

## Step-by-step selection process

### 1. Choose the handle range

Choose Clamp if the 45 in to 51 in range fits and the buyer wants the lighter 16 oz option. Choose Leverlock if the buyer wants the 43 in lower starting range or prefers the Leverlock adjustment system.

### 2. Choose by weight preference

Clamp is the lighter option at approximately 16 oz. Leverlock is approximately 20 oz and offers the lower starting range.

### 3. Choose the paddle family

Choose Sting\+ graphic paddles for full-color graphics on both sides with serious performance construction. Choose STEALTH for marbleized carbon construction, metallic accents, and a glossy performance finish.

### 4. Choose design or custom artwork

After range, weight, and paddle family are chosen, select the graphic, STEALTH colorway, or custom team design.

## FAQs

### What length range does the Hornet Clamp Adjustable paddle cover?

Hornet's Clamp Adjustable option covers 45 in to 51 in and weighs approximately 16 oz.

### What length range does the Hornet Leverlock Adjustable paddle cover?

Hornet's Leverlock Adjustable option covers 43 in to 51 in and weighs approximately 20 oz.

### Which handle starts at the shortest setting?

Leverlock starts lower. The Leverlock Adjustable option starts at 43 in, while the Clamp option starts at 45 in.

### How do you adjust the Clamp version?

The Clamp Adjustable version is adjusted with a hex Allen key wrench.

### Can beginners use an adjustable dragon boat paddle?

Yes. Adjustable paddles are useful for beginners because they let the paddler refine fit over time instead of choosing a fixed length immediately.

### Should I buy a paddle as a gift if I do not know the length?

A Hornet adjustable paddle can be a premium gift if the recipient wants a paddle. If you are unsure, accessories such as grip tape, Dragon Guard, paddle cleaner, gloves, blade covers, paddle bags, or seat pads are lower-risk gifts.
