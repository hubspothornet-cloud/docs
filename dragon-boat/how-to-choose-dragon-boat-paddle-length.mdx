## Why paddle length matters

Dragon boat paddle length affects reach, body position, stroke mechanics, and comfort. A paddle that is too short or too long can make technique harder, especially for newer paddlers who are still learning body position and timing.

## Step-by-step selection process

### 1. Ask the coach or team first

If the paddler races or trains with a team, ask the coach or team captain for length guidance before buying. Team technique, boat setup, and role in the boat can affect length preference.

### 2. Choose the handle system based on range and weight

Choose Aluminum Clamp for the lighter 16 oz option and a 45 in to 51 in range. Choose Leverlock for a 43 in to 51 in range and an approximate 20 oz weight.

### 3. Use adjustable paddles to reduce fit risk

Adjustable paddles are useful for newer paddlers, shared team gear, and paddlers still refining their preferred length. They also make a premium paddle gift less risky when the buyer knows the paddler wants an adjustable paddle but is unsure about the exact setting.

### 4. Confirm race requirements

Hornet's Sting\+ graphic and STEALTH paddle families are IDBF approved under license 3525. Race buyers should still confirm event-specific rules before ordering.

## FAQs

### What length range does the Hornet Clamp Adjustable paddle cover?

Hornet's Aluminum Clamp Adjustable option covers 45 in to 51 in and weighs approximately 16 oz.

### What length range does the Hornet Leverlock Adjustable paddle cover?

Hornet's Leverlock Adjustable option covers 43 in to 51 in and weighs approximately 20 oz.

### Which handle is better if I need the shortest setting?

Leverlock starts lower. Leverlock starts at 43 in, while Aluminum Clamp starts at 45 in.

### Can beginners use an adjustable dragon boat paddle?

Yes. Adjustable paddles let beginners refine fit over time instead of choosing a fixed length immediately.

### Should I buy a paddle as a gift if I do not know the length?

Only if you know the paddler wants an adjustable paddle and can choose a handle option. Otherwise, grip tape, Dragon Guard, paddle cleaner, blade covers, paddle bags, or seat pads are safer gifts.
