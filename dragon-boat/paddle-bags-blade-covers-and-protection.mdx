## When should you use a paddle bag?

Use a paddle bag when you want to carry and store the full paddle. The reviewed Hornet paddle bags are positioned for protecting paddles from scratches and damage, and both current product pages say the bags can fit up to 2 paddles.

A paddle bag is the better starting point when you travel with your paddle, store it with other gear, or want pockets for additional items.

## When should you use a blade cover?

Use a blade cover when you want a smaller protection option focused on the paddle blade. Both reviewed Hornet blade covers say they protect dragon boat paddles, include a back zip pocket for a cell phone, keys, and wallet, and use a nylon zipper.

The current blade cover copy also says the covers fit all IDBF approved paddles. If you use an unusually shaped or non-IDBF paddle, verify fit with Hornet before buying.

## When does Dragon Guard make sense?

Dragon Guard is the tip-protection option. The current Shopify copy is short, so buyers should confirm material and compatibility on the product page or with Hornet before making assumptions.

Dragon Guard belongs in a basic protection setup with a paddle bag or blade cover, especially when the buyer wants lower-cost paddle-tip protection.

## How should you clean a dragon boat paddle?

Start with the paddle material. Hornet Paddle Cleaner's current product copy says it is designed for carbon fiber, fiberglass, composite, and plastic paddles. It is described as a no-rinse formula: spray, wipe with a soft cloth or sponge, and for tougher stains let it sit for 60 to 90 seconds before wiping.

<Warning>
  Do not use a cleaner on a paddle material unless the product page or manufacturer confirms compatibility. Avoid making stronger chemical, safety, or finish-protection claims unless the label or source data supports them.
</Warning>
