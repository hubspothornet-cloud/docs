## When should you use a paddle bag?

Use a paddle bag when you want to carry and store the full paddle. Hornet's Black and Pink/Black full paddle bags are designed to protect paddles from scratches and damage. Both include storage pockets, fit up to two paddles, and open from the blade side.

A full paddle bag is the stronger option for travel, race-day transport, and storage with other equipment.

## When should you use a blade cover?

Use a blade cover when you want a smaller protection option focused on the blade. Hornet's blade covers include a back zip pocket for small items such as a phone, keys, or wallet, and use a nylon zipper.

The product pages state that the covers fit IDBF-approved paddles. Confirm fit before using one with an unusually shaped or non-IDBF paddle.

## When does Dragon Guard make sense?

Dragon Guard is the compact tip-protection option. It can be used by itself or combined with a paddle bag or blade cover when the paddler wants another layer of protection at the paddle tip.

## How should you clean a dragon boat paddle?

Start with the paddle material. Hornet Paddle Cleaner is designed for carbon fiber, fiberglass, composite, and plastic paddles. It removes dirt, grime, and salt residue with a no-rinse spray-and-wipe process. For tougher stains, the instructions say to let the cleaner sit for 60 to 90 seconds before wiping.

The formula is described as marine safe, biodegradable, suitable for saltwater and freshwater environments, citrus scented, and made in the USA.

<Warning>
  Confirm cleaner compatibility before use, especially on custom coatings, repaired areas, or unusual finishes. Follow the product-label instructions.
</Warning>

A paddle bag protects and stores the full paddle. A blade cover focuses on the blade and uses less space.
