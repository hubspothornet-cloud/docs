The AEO Growth Loop runs from a single prompt file — `AEO-GROWTH-LOOP-TEMPLATE.md`. Before you schedule it, you need to replace every `{{PLACEHOLDER}}` with your store's real values. The VARIABLES table at the top of the file lists every placeholder and what it expects.

## Required Placeholders

Replace all of the following before scheduling the loop. Leaving any placeholder unfilled will cause the run to fail or produce incorrect output.

| Placeholder | What to put here |
|---|---|
| `{{STORE_NAME}}` | Your store's display name (e.g., `SonoHealth`) |
| `{{STORE_DOMAIN}}` | Your Shopify domain (e.g., `sonohealth.com`) |
| `{{SHOPIFY_HANDLE}}` | Your store's Shopify handle |
| `{{DOCS_DOMAIN}}` | Your docs subdomain (e.g., `docs.sonohealth.com`) |
| `{{DOCS_REPO_PATH}}` | Absolute local path to your cloned docs repo |
| `{{DOCS_CONFIG_FILE}}` | `docs.json` or `mint.json` depending on your repo |
| `{{CORE_PRODUCT_MON}}` | Monday's focus product (Shopify handle) |
| `{{CORE_PRODUCT_TUE}}` | Tuesday's focus product (Shopify handle) |
| `{{CORE_PRODUCT_WED}}` | Wednesday's focus product (Shopify handle) |
| `{{CORE_PRODUCT_THU}}` | Thursday's focus product (Shopify handle) |
| `{{ROTATING_PRODUCTS}}` | Comma-separated handles for Friday's rotation |
| `{{RETURN_POLICY}}` | Your real return policy (e.g., `30 days, no questions asked`) |
| `{{WARRANTY}}` | Your real warranty terms |
| `{{STORE_RATING}}` | Your real average rating (e.g., `4.7`) |
| `{{REVIEW_COUNT}}` | Your real review count |

<Warning>
  Never put aspirational numbers in. The rating, review count, warranty, and return policy must be your real, verifiable ones. AI engines and legal regimes both penalize fabricated claims.
</Warning>

## Choosing Your Four Core Products

Core products (Monday through Thursday) get roughly 85% of the loop's attention each week. Choose them deliberately.

Pick products with the most **new-customer headroom** — not necessarily your current bestsellers. Good criteria:

- Products with incomplete Q&A coverage in your Knowledge Base
- Products that appear in AI shopping queries but aren't being cited
- Products with high traffic but low conversion (a gap the loop's PDP work can close)
- Products where competitors are getting cited but you aren't

<Tip>
  Check your Agentic Storefronts panel to see which products are already getting AI-agent traffic. Prioritize the ones with traffic but low citation accuracy — those are the highest-leverage targets for the loop.
</Tip>

Friday's rotating slot (`{{ROTATING_PRODUCTS}}`) is for secondary products that don't need daily attention but benefit from periodic refresh. Include three to five handles, separated by commas.

## Adapting the Vertical Block

The template ships with a **YMYL (Your Money or Your Life)** medical-safety guardrail written for health, device, and supplement stores. This block instructs the loop to qualify claims carefully, cite evidence appropriately, and never overstate efficacy.

- **If your store is in a health, device, or supplement vertical**, keep the block verbatim. It protects you legally and improves citation quality with AI engines that apply YMYL filters.
- **If your store is in a different vertical**, replace the block with the claim rules appropriate for your category. The principle is the same: calibrated, grounded, well-qualified claims earn AI citations. Overstated claims get filtered out.

Example guidance by vertical:

| Vertical | Key claim rule |
|---|---|
| Consumer electronics | Include FCC/safety certification numbers where relevant; qualify performance claims with test conditions |
| Food & beverage | Follow FDA nutrition claim guidelines; distinguish "contains" from "provides a therapeutic benefit" |
| Financial products | Include required disclosure language; qualify projections with standard risk language |
| Home goods | Cite test standards (UL, ASTM, etc.) for safety claims; qualify dimension tolerances |

If you're unsure which claim rules apply to your category, consult your legal team before running the loop at scale.
