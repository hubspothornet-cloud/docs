## Step 1: Match the cushion to the problem

Do not buy only by price. If slipping is the issue, start with an anti-slip product. If padding is the issue, start with a thicker cushion. If seat fit is the issue, compare product dimensions with your rowing machine seat.

## Step 2: Know that Hornet cushions are not only for Concept2

Hornet rowing machine cushions can fit Concept2 and many other common rowers, including Hydrow, Peloton, Merach, Sunny Health, and generic rowing machines. Seat shapes vary, so compare product dimensions and images with your machine before buying.

## Step 3: Consider thickness carefully

More padding can feel better for some rowers, but it can also change the feel of the seat. The Memory Foam Cushion is almost 2 inches thick and is the better starting point when maximum padding matters. The Anti Slip Cushion is the better starting point when grip and size options matter more than maximum padding.

## Step 4: Add gloves if grip is part of the problem

Hornet paddling gloves feature silicone grip on the palm side to reduce slipping and add security. They are useful for indoor rowing workouts when the buyer wants more hand grip or hand protection.

## Step 5: Keep technique and setup in the conversation

A cushion can help with comfort, but seat discomfort can also come from rowing volume, posture, seat position, clothing, or technique. This guide should not promise that a cushion will solve pain, numbness, injury, or technique problems.

<Warning>
  If rowing causes persistent pain, numbness, or injury concerns, do not rely on a cushion alone. Review technique, machine setup, training volume, and seek qualified coaching or medical advice where appropriate.
</Warning>
