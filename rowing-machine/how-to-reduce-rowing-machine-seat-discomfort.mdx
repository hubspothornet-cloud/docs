## Step 1: Match the cushion to the problem

Do not buy only by price. If slipping is the issue, start with an anti-slip product. If padding is the issue, start with a thicker cushion. If machine fit is the issue, start with a product whose title or description clearly references the machine.

## Step 2: Confirm Concept2 fit when needed

Hornet offers several cushions designed or described for Concept2 rowing machines. Check exact dimensions and fit on the product page before buying. Do not assume every rowing seat pad fits Concept2.

## Step 3: Consider thickness carefully

More padding can feel better for some rowers, but it can also change seat feel and rowing position. Hornet describes the Memory Foam Cushion Made for Concept2 as almost 2 inches thick. Confirm the thickness of other cushions on their product pages.

## Step 4: Check technique and setup

Seat discomfort can also relate to posture, seat position, clothing, workout volume, or technique. A cushion may help comfort, but it cannot correct every setup or movement problem.

<Warning>
  If rowing causes persistent pain, numbness, or injury concerns, do not rely on a cushion alone. Review technique, machine setup, training volume, and seek qualified coaching or medical advice where appropriate.
</Warning>
